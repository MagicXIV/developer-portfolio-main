My Experience

Methods and Statistics

Research Master

I am currently completing a research master’s degree at the University of Utrecht in Methodology and Statistics for the Behavioural, Biomedical and Social Sciences (MSBBSS) or simply Methods and Statistics.

This programme has provided me with an in-depth understanding of widely used statistical methods, including multilevel modelling, Bayesian statistics, structural equation modelling, analysing survey data, and more. You can find the official programme page here

For my electives, I chose courses at Cito, where I studied: Contemporary Datasets, Psychometrics and Data Science, and Bayesian Psychometrics. These courses allowed me to further specialise in (Bayesian) psychometrics, the use of machine learning, and working with realistic datasets resembling real-life scenarios.

Master’s Thesis

For my Masters’ thesis, I am evaluating the impact of test motivation on PISA and TIMSS Outcomes, where test motivation is measured by missing answers to questions and response times. PISA and TIMSS are both Internal Large-Scale Assessment studies (ILSAs).

Bachelor

I have previously completed my Bachelors in Psychology at the University of Twente. Alongside the standard programme, I followed the elective modules Psychology of Safety, and Human factors & Engineering Psychology, as well as completed a minor abroad in Madrid.

  • The Bachelor Psychology provided me with the basic psychology facts and thought processes helpful in both everyday life as well as for academic purposes.

  • The module Psychology of Safety taught me how to influence individual responses to risks and safety issues, such as proper crowd control when disasters occur.

  • Human factors & Engineering Psychology offered great insight into how one can implement psychological findings into designing websites, daily user objects, apps, or even cars. As a part of this module, I completed a project programming an eye-tracking device using Python, a ruler, and a cheap camera.

  • The minor was focused on companies, their structures and their management.

Thesis

My Bachelor thesis focused on the statistical analysis of electroencephalogram (EEG) trial data through Bayesian covariance structure modelling (BCSM). I conducted a simulation study for the evaluation of the BCSM compared to a linear mixed effects model for different sample sizes and covariance values. The BCSM was then applied to electroencephalogram (EEG) trial data.
